Documentation and responsibility map

A background policy map for the current electrical and EKOM workload. It narrows the app to the responsibilities, questions, evidence and sources that apply to the worker, organization, exact task, site and work phase.

Design outcome
The same map powers personal guidance, organization assignment, job checklists, evidence capture, handover and later selective proof.

How the app narrows the rule space

A free-text job request is not enough. The app progressively establishes five kinds of context and loads policy packs only after the worker confirms the classification.

Person

Verified and self-declared fagbrev, courses, practice, restrictions, role and expiry.

Organization

Employer, legal entity, active authorizations, registered scope, professional responsibility and coordination.

Task

Exact operation, discipline, system, new work, change, maintenance, test or inspection.

Context

Owner, assignment, building, worksite, environment, other trades, hazards and work method.

Rule version

Jurisdiction, effective date, official source, reviewed rule pack and unresolved uncertainty.
Output: the smallest relevant before-start, responsibility, execution, test and handover checklist
The language model may propose task and context labels from the worker's description. A deterministic router validates them, the worker confirms them, and only reviewed policy packs can create a blocker or scope result.

Documentation follows the life of the job

Responsibilities are attached to a phase. This prevents handover requirements appearing during intake or execution controls being forgotten after the initial risk assessment.

1

Intake

Describe, split and bound the assignment. Identify owner, employer, site and work packages.

2

Eligibility

Check personal competence, organization authorization, role assignment and responsibility interfaces.

3

Pre-start

Load SHA/HMS, task-specific risk, FSE, work method, instructions, equipment and evidence requirements.

4

Execution

Keep a concise work record, deviations, changes, time and evidence linked to the confirmed work package.

5

Test

Apply system and task-specific final control, test method, result, deviation and correction requirements.

6

Handover

Generate only the applicable declaration, report, as-built information and customer receipt.

7

Retention

Preserve required copies, source versions, correction history and verification commitments.

8

Incident

Protect private notes and create a worker-controlled chronology or selected evidence export.

Example: one request becomes two work packages

A customer asks for a new electrical socket and a network outlet. The app should not answer the combined request with one credential check.

Electrical package

  • Confirm fixed-installation scope.
  • Verify active registered electrical enterprise category.
  • Confirm faglig ansvarlig, assigned worker competence and ansvarlig for arbeidet.
  • Run FSE task-risk and work-method checks.
  • Load applicable design, final-control and handover evidence.
+

EKOM package

  • Classify the network activity and any authorization exception.
  • Verify responsible enterprise and ENA when required.
  • Confirm relevant installer competence.
  • Capture route, separation, category, terminations and test method.
  • Load applicable as-built, labeling and conformity evidence.
Shared interface: power method, route, separation, EMC, fire sealing, ownership and customer handover are coordinated, while authority, responsibility and sign-off remain explicit per work package.

How this saves time

The commercial value is not showing more regulations. It is reducing repeated entry, uncertainty, missing handover documents and rework.

Reuse verified factsWorker credentials, company authorizations and recurring responsible roles are checked once and refreshed when due.
Ask only the deltaThe app requests only missing or job-specific facts instead of repeating a full compliance form.
Split mixed workFree text becomes separate work packages before the worker starts, reducing wrong assumptions and handoffs.
Generate evidence listsTest and handover requirements appear while the work is being planned, not after the worker has left the site.
Prefill reportsConfirmed observations, responsibility, tests and source versions flow into the final package without duplicate entry.
Target interaction: show no more than five primary actions for the current phase. Put legal sources and detailed rationale behind a clear "Why?" view, while every blocker still names the missing fact and responsible party.

Governance before automation

The policy map becomes dangerous if it quietly goes stale. Rules need ownership, effective dates, source checks, negative tests and a visible uncertainty state.

  • Each rule has an owner, qualified reviewer, source IDs, effective date and review due date.
  • Official law, regulation, guidance, registry and licensed standard references are labeled differently.
  • Paid standards are referenced by edition and licensed section, not copied into the repository.
  • Every rule has positive, negative, boundary and uncertainty test cases.
  • Expired or changed sources disable confident approval until review is complete.
  • Completed assessments retain the exact historical map and source version.
  • The LLM cannot create or edit a production rule.
  • A domain expert approves the first electrical/EKOM pack before worker testing.
  • Workers can report an incorrect or unclear result without exposing unrelated job data.
  • Usage metrics measure time saved and correction rate, not worker productivity.
