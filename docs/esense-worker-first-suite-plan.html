A worker-first esense suite

A private assistant that helps a person understand, document and prove work. Organizations are optional. Responsible employers gain coordination and assurance later, while authorities receive only evidence deliberately disclosed under a valid purpose.

No organization required Worker core remains free Private by default Local AI only by default No public reputation score Rules remain deterministic Professional field interface

Companion artifacts: documentation and responsibility map  |  machine-readable policy-pack draft  |  first-build contract

Give every worker a private, practical way to know the expected scope of a job, document what happened, and prove selected facts without surrendering the underlying record.
Primary product decision The product core remains personal and worker-controlled. The first operational build is a professional assignment workflow inside an organization, with a school serving as the initial organization and without transferring ownership of private worker records.
Worker value

Less uncertainty and better evidence

Job guidance, credential awareness, a shift and work diary, private notes, evidence capture, handover packages and understandable explanations of official sources.

Employer value

Clearer scope and easier documentation

Optional job assignment, competence matching, templates, authorization records, documentation completeness and verifiable handover without access to private worker notes.

Public value

Evidence that can be assessed

Worker-controlled PDF and JSON packages, provenance, timestamps, corrections and selective proofs. No authority receives a live feed into worker activity.

Adoption without an organization gate

The first useful action must not require an employer invitation, organization number or administrative setup. Organization features are an upgrade path, not the entrance fee.

1

Try privately

Use a local-only job note or scope check before creating an account. The app explains what will remain on the device.

2

Personal account

Sync an encrypted personal vault, create a worker profile and retain records across devices. No organization is required.

3

Credential wallet

Add fagbrev, courses, certificates and experience claims. Clearly distinguish self-declared, reviewed and registry-verified evidence.

4

Optional organization

Accept an invitation or create a company workspace. Share only selected job and credential facts through explicit grants.

5

Assurance and export

Create a customer handover, employer proof or authority evidence package. The recipient verifies only the disclosed claim.

Professional by use, not decoration

esense must feel credible to a technician, teacher, responsible enterprise, customer and inspector. Visual polish matters, but professional quality is earned through predictable behavior, precise language, traceable decisions and reliable records in real field conditions.

Field ergonomics

Fast with one hand and poor connectivity

Large touch targets, short questions, camera-first evidence, visible save and sync state, offline drafts, no horizontal scrolling and no critical action hidden behind an icon.

Professional trust

Every status has a defined meaning

Draft, worker-confirmed, supervisor-reviewed, issued, corrected and cryptographically verified are separate states. A green state never means more than the system can prove.

Record quality

Evidence remains tied to scope and time

Photos, measurements, comments, sources, revisions and responsible roles belong to the exact work package. Exports preserve authorship, timestamps, source versions and correction history.

Professional assignment pilot in a school organization

The pilot uses the same product and role model intended for professional work. The school is an organization, the teacher is a task provider and reviewer, and students are assigned workers. Only the execution context identifies the work as training or supervised practice.

Organization

School now, enterprise later

The organization owns assignment templates and submitted organization records. It never gains access to unrelated personal drafts or a worker's private vault.

Task provider

Define and assign professional work

Create the task brief, select execution context, identify known systems and constraints, assign people and roles, set due dates and name the expected plan deliverable.

Assigned worker

Plan and prepare the assigned job

Confirm the task, answer situational questions, review relevant regulations and standards, identify missing facts, prepare controls and submit a versioned plan.

Reviewer

Assess the submitted plan

Review scope, responsibility, source use, risk controls and documentation readiness. Return structured comments without opening private working notes.

Professional responsibility

Remain separate from assignment

Being assigned a task never proves competence, authorization or professional responsibility. Those claims require their own verified role and organization context.

Builder role

Keep one product model

Do not fork a school edition. Improve the professional workflow from observed use and keep training labels as context, not as a different interface.

Professional release checks

  • Complete the primary workflow at 360 px width, on a tablet and on desktop without overlap or horizontal scrolling.
  • Test keyboard navigation, focus order, screen-reader labels, zoom to 200%, color contrast and error identification.
  • Test weak network, full offline use, interrupted upload, stale data and two-device conflict recovery.
  • Print and export a report that remains readable in grayscale and clearly identifies draft, exercise and issued states.
  • Run a language review with students and professionals; prefer established Norwegian trade terms over product language.
  • Record every safety-related defect as a release blocker, not an ordinary backlog item.

First build: assigned-job planning

The first build covers the period from task creation through an accepted preparation plan. It is built as professional software. The school pilot uses the same organization, assignment, rule and review model as an electrical or ekom enterprise.

1

Create assignment

The task provider records purpose, location type, known installation or system, expected outcome, attachments, deadline and execution context.

2

Assign roles

Assign worker, reviewer and any named professionally responsible role separately. Missing responsibility remains a visible gap.

3

Accept and clarify

The assigned worker accepts, declines or asks for clarification before planning. Acceptance records who saw which assignment version.

4

Classify work

The worker confirms elektro, ekom or multiple work packages and answers deterministic situational questions.

5

Review considerations

esense shows relevant source-backed considerations, responsible actor, reason for relevance, required clarification and expected evidence.

6

Prepare plan

Document work method, risk controls, tools, materials, measurements, isolation, tests, handover and unresolved assumptions.

7

Submit version

Freeze a reviewable submission. Private notes stay private; the organization receives only the submitted assignment response and selected evidence.

8

Review and return

The reviewer accepts or returns the plan with structured findings. Corrections create a new linked version.

State model

Assignment

Draft to closed

draft, published, accepted, declined, in planning, submitted, returned, accepted plan, cancelled, closed.

Submission

Versioned review record

working copy, submitted v1, under review, returned, submitted v2, accepted, superseded.

Synchronization

Separate technical truth

local, queued, syncing, synced, failed, conflict. Sync state never changes professional review state.

First-build boundary: complete planning and review well before adding execution, final inspection, customer handover, payment, public proof or government integration. The slice succeeds when an assigned person understands what must be considered and can submit a defensible preparation plan with fewer omissions.

Three separate data boundaries

Joining a company cannot silently convert personal worker data into company property. Sharing is a deliberate, reversible capability with a purpose, recipient and expiry.

Personal vault

Owned by the worker. Credentials, private diary, personal notes, draft evidence and disclosure history. Not visible to organization administrators.

→

Organization workspace

Company jobs, assignments, approved templates and business records. Access is role-based and limited to the organization.

→

Disclosure package

A purpose-bound selection sent to a customer, union, lawyer, auditor or authority. No recipient receives ongoing access by default.

Ownership

Documents are not payment

esense does not sell documents, train models on them, or use hidden records to create public rankings. Workers and organizations can export their respective data.

Safety

No silent reporting

The app may prepare an evidence package, but it cannot contact an employer, authority or third party without a clear final confirmation or a separately established legal process.

Minimization

No continuous surveillance

No continuous GPS, hidden productivity monitoring, worker scoring or public negative records. Collect only what the chosen worker feature requires.

Competence and scope engine

The engine provides sourced decision support, not a legal verdict. A fagbrev alone is not a universal permission token. The result depends on the person, company, responsibility arrangement, exact task, context and rule version.

1. Person
Fagbrev, courses, practice, restrictions, verification state and expiry.
2. Organization
Active registrations, ENA, responsible person, declared work categories and location.
3. Assignment
Employer, role, supervision, responsible party, customer and project.
4. Task
Exact operation, system, voltage, network type, new work, maintenance or inspection.
5. Rule context
Jurisdiction, official source, version, effective date, exceptions and uncertainty.

Example: stikkontakt plus network outlet

A request that sounds like one small job may contain two regulated task families. Electrical work on a fixed installation must be taken on by an active registered electrical business. Installation or maintenance of an ekom network may require an Nkom-authorized organization and relevant personnel competence, subject to the exact network and any applicable exception.

The app must split the request into separate tasks, verify both the worker and organization context, cite current official sources, and escalate uncertainty. It must not infer that an electrical fagbrev automatically grants the organization ENA or that every network-related task has the same authorization requirement.

Deployment

Replicated small workers

Run independent quantized models on several modest computers and route complete tasks to an available node. Do not begin by sharding one model across unreliable devices.

Isolation

Stateless and organization-scoped

One task context at a time, no shared retrieval namespace, no prompt-content logs, no training on user data, temporary files in memory and explicit deletion after response.

Fallback

The product works when AI is down

Forms, task catalogs, credential checks, calculations, receipts and exports remain available. AI failure may reduce convenience but cannot block documentation.

Approved AI roles: translation, task extraction, document classification, missing-item suggestions, report drafting and explanation from curated official sources. Prohibited roles include final legal conclusions, automatic reporting, public reputation scoring and autonomous disclosure.

Midnight as private assurance

Midnight is not a company score or public activity ledger. It is a way to prove a narrow selected claim while private records remain off-chain.

Useful claims

Proof without publication

  • A package existed before a given handover time.
  • The selected package has not changed.
  • Required evidence categories existed for a template version.
  • A credential or authorization claim was valid on a date.
  • A disclosed calculation used committed source records.
Ledger boundary

Commitments only

  • Use random salts and one-time capability references.
  • Do not publish worker, company, customer or site identifiers.
  • Do not make all jobs linkable to a global company key.
  • Keep documents, model prompts and evidence off-chain.
Honest limits

Integrity is not truth

A proof can establish that committed inputs satisfy a defined calculation. It cannot prove that physical work was safe or that a self-entered statement was true. Strong claims need trusted issuers, registry checks or multiple signatures.

The existing esense receipt is phase-zero infrastructure. Before external assurance, migrate from server-only HMAC verification to public-key signatures, append-only events, salted commitments and the isolated Midnight Preprod pilot. No mainnet funds are required during validation.

Government-compatible, not government-controlled

The first public-sector feature is an evidence package that a worker can understand and choose to disclose. Direct authority integration comes only after a formal partner confirms the package is useful and the legal basis is clear.

Free worker core, paid coordination

Charging a vulnerable worker for basic rights protection weakens adoption and purpose. Organizations and integrations should finance the shared infrastructure.

Free

Personal worker

Personal account, credential wallet, work diary, basic scope checks, private evidence, receipts, export and a fair local-assistant allowance.

Paid

Organization

Team assignments, authorizations, shared templates, storage, approvals, integrations, audit reports, dedicated local inference and support.

Contracted

Enterprise and public sector

Private project assurance, procurement APIs, managed deployment, retention policies, service levels and formally approved authority integrations.

Verification remains free. A customer, union, lawyer or inspector should not need to pay esense to verify a receipt or selectively disclosed proof.

Phased roadmap and gates

Do not build execution, government integration or a broad trade library before assigned workers repeatedly produce useful preparation plans.

Phase 1

Professional assignment planning

Organization, task provider, assigned worker, execution context, assignment version, work-package classification, source considerations, preparation plan, deliberate submission and structured review.

Quality gate: at least 20 classroom jobs across elektro and ekom complete with zero data loss and no safety-critical omission caused by the interface. Market gate: 10 independent workers use it weekly for four weeks; students do not count toward this gate.
Phase 2

One competence boundary

Build one expert-reviewed domain pack, recommended pilot: electrical plus ekom task separation. Include sources, dates and uncertainty escalation.

Gate: a qualified reviewer accepts the engine output on a representative test set with no unsafe automatic approvals.
Phase 3

Local AI assistance

Task extraction, translation, missing-item suggestions and report drafts through the isolated local worker queue.

Gate: useful acceptance rate, understandable corrections and no cross-context leakage in security tests.
Phase 4

Organization operations

Reusable assignment templates, organization authorizations, supervision, capacity views and selective credential sharing without exposing the personal vault.

Gate: two responsible employers use the workflow and at least one is willing to pay for coordination.
Phase 5

Private assurance

Public-key receipts, capability links, salted commitments, revocation and Midnight Preprod proofs for selected claims.

Gate: workers and recipients understand exactly what is and is not proven.
Phase 6

Authority-reviewed export

Ask a union, Fair Play organization, lawyer or Arbeidstilsynet contact to assess a synthetic evidence package before any integration.

Gate: a named external reviewer confirms that the package reduces assessment effort and has an appropriate legal basis.

Contract for the AI that builds it

These requirements are product invariants. A builder may propose alternatives, but must not silently weaken them.

  • A person can use the core product without creating or joining an organization.
  • Joining an organization never exposes the personal vault or private diary.
  • A worker may belong to multiple organizations with separate grants and views.
  • School and enterprise organizations use the same role and assignment model.
  • Teacher and student are pilot mappings, not hard-coded product permissions.
  • Task provider, assigned worker, reviewer and professionally responsible are independent roles.
  • Assignment never implies competence, authorization, supervision or professional responsibility.
  • Credentials retain source, verification state, issuer, expiry and history.
  • Organization authorization is modeled separately from individual competence.
  • Every scope assessment cites source, rule version, effective date and confidence.
  • Every visible status has a documented meaning and never implies more assurance than the system can prove.
  • Offline, queued, synchronized, failed and conflicted records are visibly distinct.
  • The interface uses semantic controls, visible focus, persistent labels and tested keyboard and screen-reader order.
  • Exercise records are unmistakable and never presented as authorization, competence or completed professional work.
  • The deterministic scope engine works when all LLM workers are unavailable.
  • LLM workers have no direct database access and receive minimum scoped inputs.
  • No prompt or document is used for model training or retained in inference logs.
  • Every external disclosure requires purpose, recipient, fields and expiry.
  • No public worker or company score, blacklist or failed-proof history is created.
  • No personal or company identifiers are written to Midnight.
  • Corrections append history; they do not silently rewrite issued receipts.
  • Authority export is worker-controlled until a formally reviewed legal process exists.
  • The current esense production app and its data are backed up and preserved during v2 development.
  • All migrations, tenant boundaries, proof claims and rule decisions receive automated tests.

Red lines

  • No continuous location tracking or hidden worker productivity monitoring.
  • No automatic report to an employer, authority or third party.
  • No LLM-generated legal permission presented as authoritative.
  • No company administrator override into a worker's personal vault.
  • No blockchain deployment merely because the proof server is available.
  • No broad government dashboard into personal activity.

Decisions required before implementation

First worker group

Choose one fag and a small representative group. Electrical and ekom is a strong boundary test, but requires expert participation.

First daily action

Decide whether the narrow entry feature is scope check, job record or work diary. It should save time on day one.

Credential verification

Define which claims are self-declared, manually reviewed, registry-derived or issuer-signed.

Local-only trial

Decide how much can be used without an account and how encrypted recovery works after account creation.

Pilot partner

Identify a worker advocate, union, responsible employer or domain expert who can review safety and usefulness.

Language order

Select the first worker languages based on pilot users, not assumptions, and test all safety-critical explanations with humans.
